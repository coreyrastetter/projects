Visiting Boston!
In no particular order, below is a list of some of Boston's most favorite sites. The lists contain many free and/or low cost tourist attractions that will make your stay in Massachusetts memorable.

CAMBRIDGE

Freedom Trailwww.thefreedomtrail.org
Visit the many historic areas of Boston by way of the Freedom Trail. The Freedom Trail is a 2.5-mile, brick-lined route that leads you to 16 historically significant sites - each one an authentic treasure. Explore museums and meetinghouses, churches, and burying grounds. Learn about the brave people who shaped our nation. Discover the rich history of the American Revolution, as it began in Boston, where every step tells a story. Many of our favorite Freedom Trail spots are mentioned below.

Harvard Yard and Harvard Squarewww.harvard.edu
Harvard Yard is a grassy area of 22.4 acres enclosed by fences with 27 gates, the oldest part of the Harvard University campus and its historic center and modern crossroads.

Harvard University is a private Ivy League research university in Cambridge, Massachusetts, whose history, influence and wealth has made it one of the most prestigious universities in the world.

Massachusetts Institute of Technology (MIT)www.mit.edu
The Massachusetts Institute of Technology (MIT) is a private research university in Cambridge, known traditionally for research and education in the physical sciences and engineering, and more recently in biology, economics, linguistics, and management as well.

Central Kitchenwww.enormous.tv/central/index1.html
A renovated take-out wings joint in Central Square, devoted to rustic Mediterranean style.

Brick and Mortarwww.brickandmortarbar.com
Located above Central Kitchen, Brick and Mortar is worth the trip if you're in need of a craft cocktail.

Paul Revere's Housewww.paulreverehouse.org
The Paul Revere House built in 1680 was the colonial home of American patriot Paul Revere during the time of the American Revolution.

Old North Churchwww.oldnorth.com
The Old North Church is the location from which the famous "One if by land, and two if by sea" signal is said to have been sent. This phrase is related to Paul Revere's midnight ride, of April 18, 1775, which preceded the Battles of Lexington and Concord during the American Revolution.

Christopher Columbus Waterfront Park (located in Boston's North End)www.bostonharborwalk.com
Christopher Columbus Park includes broad lawns, a play lot, a spray fountain and performance area, a statue of Christopher Columbus, and a wisteria covered trellis that provides shade during the day and is lit during the holiday season. The park is an oasis in the North End and is an excellent place to relax and enjoy the harbor.

Faneuil Hallwww.faneuilhallmarketplace.com
Faneuil Hall, located near the waterfront and today's Government Center, in Boston, Massachusetts, has been a marketplace and a meeting hall since 1742.

Frost Ice Barwww.frosticebar.com
Experience the coolest thing to do in Boston; New England's only Ice Bar in Faneuil Hall, Boston.

Improve Asylumwww.improvasylum.com
The Improv Asylum is an improvisational comedy theater in the North End neighborhood of Boston, Massachusetts, United States.

New England Aquariumwww.neaq.org
Founded in 1969, the New England Aquarium is a global leader in ocean exploration and marine conservation.

Museum of Sciencewww.mos.org
The Boston Museum of Science features a revolving schedule of temporary exhibits, IMAX films, and Planetarium shows, plus details on more than 700 interactive permanent exhibits and live demonstrations.

Sauswww.eatfrites.com
Fantasic Belgian street food!

Union Oyster Housewww.unionoysterhouse.com
Union Oyster House is Boston's oldest restaurant, serving seafood and New England specialties in a historic setting on the Freedom Trail.

The Bell in Hand Tavernbellinhand.com
Established in 1795, Bell in Hand claims to be America's oldest tavern.

Anthem Kitchen and Barwww.anthem-boston.com
Chic neighborhood restaurant tucked in the historic heart of Boston's Faneuil Hall. We recommend grabbing a cup of their award winning chowder!
